# Script #2 "GlobeGenius vs. Manual Search" — VERSION RÉVISÉE

**Platform:** Facebook / Instagram Feed  
**Duration:** 25–28s  
**Language:** French  
**Target audience:** Frequent travelers, Skyscanner/Google Flights users (25–40 yo)  

---

## 🎬 Scene Breakdown with Psychology

### Scene 1: Hook (0–2s) — **Solution-First + Contrast**

**Voiceover (normal, matter-of-fact):**
> "J'ai passé 2h sur Skyscanner. Rien. GlobeGenius? 30 secondes. 5 deals."

**On screen:**
- Split screen: left shows frustrated creator staring at laptop (Skyscanner open)
- Right side: clock spinning through 2 hours ⏱️
- Text overlay: "2H OF SEARCHING" vs "30 SECONDS"

**Psychology applied:**
- **Solution-first:** Opens with SOLUTION, not problem
- **Contrast Effect:** 2h vs 30s makes the comparison visceral
- **Specificity:** "5 deals" is concrete proof (not "some deals")
- **Pain-to-relief arc:** Frustration → immediate solution
- **Anchoring:** Time investment (2h) anchors the value of time-saving

---

### Scene 2: Proof & Speed (2–8s) — **Availability Heuristic + Hyperbolic Discounting**

**Voiceover (energetic, slightly amazed):**
> "Hier, Istanbul à 45€. Réservé direct. Ça disparaît vite, ces deals."

**On screen:**
- Quick montage (2–3s each):
  - Laptop showing GlobeGenius notification pop-up
  - Price displayed: "Istanbul €45"
  - Booking confirmation screen (blur personal data)
  - Success checkmark animation

**Psychology applied:**
- **Availability Heuristic:** Concrete example (Istanbul €45) makes the deal feel real and achievable
- **Proof moment:** Booking confirmation = "This actually happened"
- **Speed proof:** "Réservé direct" = no thinking time needed
- **Scarcity:** "Ça disparaît vite" = urgency
- **Hyperbolic Discounting:** Immediate action (booking) > delayed benefit (trip)

---

### Scene 3: Why Manual Search Fails (8–14s) — **Rational explanation**

**Voiceover (conversational):**
> "Avec Skyscanner tu dois checker toi-même. Tous les jours. Les erreurs tarifaires? Elles durent 30 minutes, max. Tu les rates."

**On screen:**
- Montage showing the problem:
  - Creator clicking through Skyscanner repeatedly (boring)
  - Calendar showing "Day 1... Day 5..." repeated checks
  - Notification showing "Deal expired" ❌
  - Text overlay: "30-MINUTE WINDOW"

**Psychology applied:**
- **Loss Aversion:** "Tu les rates" = concrete loss (missed opportunity)
- **Contrast:** Manual daily work vs. automated alerts
- **Legitimacy:** "30 minutes max" = explains why they never find deals themselves
- **Confirmation:** Validates the user's frustration ("I'm not crazy for struggling")

---

### Scene 4: The Nomad Solution (14–22s) — **Social Proof + Identity Alignment**

**Voiceover (warm, encouraging):**
> "Les nomades, les voyageurs fréquents—on n'a pas le temps. GlobeGenius travaille pendant qu'on dors."

**On screen:**
- Montage showing lifestyle:
  - Creator sleeping (notification arrives while asleep)
  - Creator at beach/café (not worried about deals)
  - Phone notification pings (deal found)
  - Creator wakes up, sees deal, smiles
  - Quick booking on phone

**Psychology applied:**
- **Mimetic Desire:** "Nomads love this" implied through lifestyle
- **Identity alignment:** "On n'a pas le temps" = speaks to nomadic constraint
- **Authority:** "Works while you sleep" = 24/7 automation > human effort
- **Social proof:** Lifestyle montage = "Real people like you use this"
- **Reciprocity:** Free service (algorithm working) creates obligation

---

### Scene 5: CTA (22–25s) — **Permission-Based + Zero Friction**

**Voiceover (conversational, inviting):**
> "Gratuit. Zéro commissions. Lien en bio."

**On screen:**
- Text fades in: "GLOBEGENIUS.APP | FREE | LIEN EN BIO"
- GlobeGenius logo + brand colors
- Quick stat: "Join 50K+ travelers"

**Psychology applied:**
- **Zero-Price Effect:** "Gratuit" is psychologically different from "cheap"
- **Removal of objections:** "Zéro commissions" = addresses hidden-fee fear
- **Social Proof:** "Join 50K+" = bandwagon effect
- **Low activation energy:** "Lien en bio" = minimal friction
- **Reciprocity:** Free tier removes purchase pressure

## 🎯 Key Improvements vs. Original

1. **Flipped to solution-first:** "Skyscanner fails → GlobeGenius solves" (not "Searching is hard")
2. **Added concrete proof:** Istanbul €45 instead of generic "you'll find deals"
3. **Explained the scarcity:** "30-minute window" makes urgency rational, not manufactured
4. **Added speed contrast:** "2h vs 30 seconds" = instant credibility
5. **Nomadic identity:** "On n'a pas le temps" = speaks directly to target pain
6. **Automation authority:** "Works while you sleep" = 24/7 advantage over humans

## 🎬 Production Notes

**Scenes to film:**
1. Frustrated creator with Skyscanner (authentic annoyance = relatable)
2. Clock spinning (visual metaphor for wasted time)
3. GlobeGenius notification + booking confirmation (proof)
4. Repeated calendar checks (problem visualization)
5. Lifestyle montage: sleeping → beach → notification → smile (solution payoff)

**Color grading:** Slightly desaturated for Skyscanner scenes (boring/frustration), vibrant for GlobeGenius scenes (solution)

**Pacing:** Fast cuts in problem sections (2–14s), slower, peaceful cuts in solution section (14–22s) = visual metaphor for automation
